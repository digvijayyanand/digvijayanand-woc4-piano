  1. Playing Piano instructions

    c3: ‘q’ , c#3: ‘2’ , d3: ‘w’ , d#3: ‘3’….and so on till e4:‘p’ after that, press ‘z’ onwards, f4: ‘z’, f#4: ’s’..so on till g#5: ‘;’ a6: ‘/’

  2. Stopwatch

    Stopwatch is as easy as it looks, a standard stopwatch.

  3. Time spent

    At the time of log out, it will be shown to you the total time spent by you on home page, i.e the time for which you have played piano.

  4. Personal Guide & Recording

    A personal guide is available (at the right of the “stop recording” option in white colour), which will guide you in recording the music(and its notes). Personal help is only available on larger screens like laptops and tablets, not phones.
    Once you record and save your notes, both notes in text form and audio form will automatically be saved; you can access either or both on the saved music slide. There is play and pause feature available for recorded notes.

  5. Self-help notes

    There’s an extra feature of Self-help notes, which you can add in the "add notes slide"(you can find it by clicking add notes on the nav bar at the top), and it will be displayed on the home page. The reason why I added this feature is that at times, one may forget notes of music while playing; this feature lets you save notes in text form in advance and later, you can take help of the same note while playing the piano.
    You can access your saved notes by clicking on "Saved Self-help Notes" at the side of guidelines.(you have the option of switching the display of guidelines and Self-help notes). This feature is also only available only on large screens.
